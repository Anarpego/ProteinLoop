# Feature Spec: Judge-Winning Experience

## Goal

Turn the protein-first operator view into a competition-ready proof surface: a judge can see the food impact, watch the whole loop communicate state, execute the verifier boundary in one click, distinguish the live Gemma path from the deterministic rehearsal, and understand the project's hardware and AMD portability without opening advanced panels.

## Judge Value

Within the first viewport, a judge can answer five questions:

1. What food output is protected?
2. What changes when water becomes dangerous?
3. What does Gemma do versus the deterministic verifier?
4. Can the unsafe-action claim be executed and inspected?
5. Which parts are live now, physically evidenced, or portable to AMD?

## Functional Requirements

1. The connected food-loop strip shall animate fish and prawns, plant filtration, duckweed feed, and chickens and eggs as one ordered flow.
2. The loop animation shall respond visibly and textually to stable, at-risk, and recovered states.
3. The impact headline shall update from live simulator chemistry and verified recovery results, including the fish-and-prawn biomass affected.
4. A compact first-viewport proof ribbon shall expose configured Gemma, the five-agent team, deterministic verification, physical two-board DECT NR+ evidence, producer approval, and the portable AMD ROCm/vLLM profile.
5. AMD language shall explicitly describe a portable deployment profile and shall not imply that the current local demo is AMD-hosted.
6. A primary `Run one-click verifier proof` command shall reuse the existing deterministic demo cascade.
7. The one-click result shall visibly prove the emergency, the rejected unsafe proposal, the admitted safe recovery, zero unsafe actions executed, and measured final chemistry.
8. The verifier proof shall explicitly direct judges to the separate live Gemma recovery workflow for model-backed planning.
9. The existing manual emergency and live Gemma mission shall remain the primary immersive interaction and shall not be duplicated by a second agent runtime.
10. Dynamic impact and proof results shall use polite live regions, semantic ordered lists, keyboard-accessible controls, and text in addition to color.
11. Motion shall stop under `prefers-reduced-motion`, and mobile controls shall meet a 44-pixel minimum target without overlap.
12. Operator and producer authorization boundaries shall remain unchanged.

## Acceptance Criteria

1. LiveView tests prove stable, risk, verifier-proof, and live-Gemma recovery stories.
2. LiveView tests execute the injected demo-cascade dependency and render the three-stage verifier proof.
3. Source tests prove ordered loop animation, phase styling, reduced-motion handling, live regions, and mobile target sizing.
4. Existing Phoenix, Python, producer-permission, simulator, verifier, and harness tests pass.
5. Production assets, Docker smoke, and live-demo validation pass with the new judge-facing markers.
6. Desktop and mobile screenshots are captured when a browser backend is available; unavailable tooling is reported rather than inferred.

## Non-Goals

- The one-click verifier proof does not claim to call Gemma; it is an explicitly deterministic rehearsal.
- The UI does not claim current AMD hosting without an AMD cloud deployment.
- The feature does not add a database, analytics framework, second simulator, or second agent workflow.
- The UI does not estimate meals, nutrition, avoided mortality, or commercial yield without validated models.

## Competition Alignment

- **Creativity:** one living 3D protein loop with state-driven food-flow communication.
- **Product potential:** literal biomass-at-risk and connected food outputs rather than generic telemetry.
- **Completeness:** executable unsafe rejection, safe recovery, live Gemma workflow, producer control, and hardware evidence.
- **AMD credibility:** a visible ROCm/vLLM deployment profile described as portable, not falsely presented as the current host.
